CHAPTER IWelcome to the World

What is Battle of Origins?

Battle of Origins is a multiplayer Spiritkin RPG set in the Overworld -- a vast realm spanning frozen peaks, rolling meadows, volcanic islands, and a cursed dark castle. You play as a Warden: someone who bonds with Spiritkin (spirit creatures), explores the land, crafts gear, builds settlements, and battles alongside friends in real-time.

The game combines dice-based combat, deep talent trees, a player-driven economy, and a living world with day/night cycles, rotating zone quality, world bosses, and a mystery quest that unfolds in real time.

How to Play

⚔ Core Loop
  1. Explore -- Walk the world map, discover zones, gather materials, chase spirit wisps.
  2. Battle -- Encounter wild Spiritkin. Roll dice. Deploy resources. Win to earn loot and XP.
  3. Craft -- Bring essences to a workshop. Assemble gear. Experiment for quality bonuses.
  4. Build -- Claim land, construct houses, gardens, taverns, and more.
  5. Grow -- Invest talent points, level professions, unlock advanced classes.
  6. Collaborate -- Team up for world bosses, trade gear, visit player settlements.

CHAPTER IIThe World Map

The Overworld spans a 110x85 tile map. Each tile is 32 pixels. The world is divided into four distinct regions, each with its own hub town, encounter zones, terrain, and atmosphere.

The Four Regions

❄ Frost Valley

Snow-covered forests, frozen lakes, towering mountains, and the northern dungeon. Home to Polaris, the first hub town. The mountain range guards Crystal Caverns and a frozen waterfall. This is where every Warden begins their journey.

Hub: Polaris | Landmarks: The Mountain, Ice Dungeon, Crystal Caverns, Frozen Waterfall, Aurora Fields, Logging Camp

🌱 Rolling Hills

Gentle meadows, sun-drenched flower fields, and the finest Healing Seeds in the Overworld. Meadowbrook is a quiet farming community with an herbalist and the best crafting herbs.

Hub: Meadowbrook | Key NPCs: Farmer Bea, Herbalist Sage

🌋 Volcanic Isles

Tropical islands of fire and water. Lava rivers, magma pools, obsidian fields, and deep ocean trenches. The purest Flame Cores and strongest Potency essences come from here. Beautiful but deadly.

Hub: Volcanic Settlement | Key NPCs: Captain Flint, Lava Tender

🌈 Dark Castle

A cursed domain where Valkin the Grand's power echoes through the walls. The Spiritkin here are darker, angrier, and more dangerous. Shadow Wardens patrol the passages, and a Cursed Scholar studies the lingering magic.

Hub: Dark Castle Outpost | Key NPCs: Shadow Warden, Cursed Scholar

Zone Quality System

Every encounter zone has a quality rating that rotates on a 12-hour cycle. Higher quality means better essence drops when you defeat Spiritkin. Elder Frost in Polaris will tell you which zones are running hot or cold this cycle.

Quality Multipliers

Zone quality multiplies the base stats (potency, stability, resonance, purity) of essences you collect. Always check the zone report before grinding -- a high-quality zone can make the difference between Basic and Mastercraft gear.

CHAPTER IIICombat System

Dice Mechanics

Combat in Battle of Origins revolves around rolling dice. Each side rolls 3d6 (three six-sided dice) as a base. The dice are sorted and classified by their highest matching set:

Roll TypeRequirementDamagePresentation
SinglesNo matching dice1Muted gold -- "Six High"
Doubles2 matching dice2Gold glow -- "DOUBLES - FIVES!"
Triples3 matching dice3Gold flash + screen shake
Quads4 matching dice4Amber burst + heavy shake
Penta5 matching dice5Pale gold shimmer + eruption
Hex+6+ matching diceN (count)Rainbow cascade
Straight1-2-3-4-5-6+2 bonusSpecial announce
🎲 Tiebreakers

When two rolls tie on type, the matched value breaks the tie (doubles of 5 beats doubles of 3). If still tied, remaining dice are compared highest-to-lowest. True ties result in no damage to either side.

Cinematic Luck System

The dice engine has subtle cinematic nudges that make tight moments more exciting -- not unfair, just dramatic:

  • 1 HP: 25% chance of forced doubles (value 3-6) -- clutch comeback energy
  • 2 HP: 15% chance of forced doubles
  • 5+ dice: 10% penta nudge -- reward the player for earning extra dice
  • Low HP quality boost: 30% chance to reroll the lowest die if below average

Roll Announcer

Every roll gets a dramatic presentation. Singles are quiet taps. Doubles glow gold with a hit sound. Triples flash the screen and shake. Quads erupt. Pentas shimmer and hold for two full seconds. The announcer system turns every roll into a cinematic moment.

CHAPTER IVSpiritkin

Spiritkin are the spirit creatures of the Overworld. Each Spiritkin has unique abilities that trigger at different phases of combat.

Rarity Tiers

Common Uncommon Rare Ghost-Rare Legendary

Ability Phases

  • Entry Effects -- Trigger when a Spiritkin enters battle (swap-in or start of fight)
  • Pre-Roll Effects -- Activate before dice are rolled (commit resources, modify dice count)
  • Win-Path Effects -- Activate when you win a round (bonus damage, healing, resource gain)
  • Loss-Path Effects -- Activate when you lose a round (damage reduction, counterattacks, survival)

CHAPTER VTalent Trees

The talent system defines your role in the world. Scaling costs: Tier 0 = 1pt, Tier 1 = 2pt, Tier 2 = 3pt, Tier 3 = 4pt. Each tree has 3 branches with 4 tiers. Cap: 120 points.

Scaling Costs

Tier 0 = 1 point, Tier 1 = 2 points, Tier 2 = 3 points, Tier 3 = 4 points. Each tree has 3 branches with 4 tiers. You can master ~2 full trees and get ~85% of a third with the 120-point cap.

Hidden Classes

☠ Dark Rider

A mysterious transformation. Two unlock paths:

  1. Fortune Path: Give 100 Bad Fortunes as a Fortune Teller with Dark Fortune branch maxed.
  2. Scientist Path: Grief 10 players via lab overloads (the Spiritkin escapes and attacks nearby players).

Dark Riders can transform for 1 minute (1-day cooldown), move 50% faster, challenge live players, and access hidden Dark Rider-only areas. Branches: Shadow, Dominion, Dread. Advanced trees: Shadow Knight, Nightmare, Soul Collector.

★ Elder

Unlocked through Scholar mastery. Decipher all 4 ancient lore tablets to begin the Elder path. Elders are protectors and leaders:

  • Elder Barrier -- AoE: ward Dark Riders, defeat dark wights, heal all nearby to full HP (2-hour cooldown)
  • Elder Sanctum -- Access a unique area only Elders can visit
  • Council Voice -- Vote in the weekly Elder Council on world governance
  • Proposal Rights -- Propose amendments for the Council to vote on

CHAPTER VIICrafting & Gathering

Workshop Crafting

Crafting happens at workshops. You select a schematic, fill essence slots, assemble, and optionally experiment for bonus quality. Four quality tiers determine gear power:

QualityDescription
BasicLow-quality output. Minimal stats.
StandardDecent gear. Reliable for most content.
SuperiorHigh-quality. Noticeable stat advantage.
MastercraftBest possible. Requires high mastery + great essences.

Schematics

Essence Subtypes

Each zone produces essences with unique subtype bonuses. Essences have four stats: Potency, Stability, Resonance, and Purity. Your Spiritkin's rarity adds a base bonus (Common: 0, Uncommon: +100, Rare: +200, Ghost-Rare: +350, Legendary: +500). Scouts get an additional +100 bonus.

Gathering

Three ways to gather materials in the field:

  • Spirit Wisps -- Glowing orbs that spawn near encounter zones. Chase and collect them for crafting materials (Frost Shards, Ember Dust, Spirit Thread, Mask Fragments). Up to 8 active at once, 12-second lifetime.
  • Resource Nodes -- Static harvest points in zones.
  • Friendly Spiritkin -- Some Spiritkin can be befriended for materials instead of battled.

CHAPTER VIIIThe Garden

Gardens are player-placed structures where you grow plants for battle resources. Requires the Cultivator apprentice talent to build.

Plant Types

Growth Stages

Plants progress through 5 stages mapped to 8 sprite frames:

  1. Seed (planted)
  2. Sprout (2 min)
  3. Growing (5 min)
  4. Mature (8 min)
  5. Harvestable (10 min)

Watering speeds growth. Spirit pets (Shamans) tend gardens passively. Shaman meditation at a garden grants +1 die buff and can spawn rare spirits nearby.

CHAPTER IXBuilding & Housing

Structure Types

House Plots

There are 7 claimable house plots across all regions. Each costs 100 coins to claim. You can only own one house at a time. Demolishing refunds 50 coins.

Trophies

Display up to 5 trophies in your house. Trophies are earned from achievements, world boss kills, quest completions, and exploration milestones. Other players can visit your house and see your trophy collection.

CHAPTER XNPCs & Encounters

Friendly NPCs

Hostile Trainers

Hostile NPCs roam encounter zones and patrol specific areas. They have full teams of Spiritkin and scale by region difficulty.

Black Riders

☠ Night Patrol

Black Riders are powerful hostile NPCs that spawn exclusively during the night phase of the day/night cycle. They patrol all regions, challenge players on sight, and have full competitive teams. Players with Bad Fortunes (from the Dark Fortune branch) attract Black Riders 20% more often.

Defeating a Black Rider grants significant rewards. Elders with the Elder Barrier ability can ward them away in an area.

World Boss System

World bosses spawn on 30-minute cycles, backed by Firebase for real-time multiplayer. Multiple players contribute damage via atomic transactions. Boss drops include legendary schematics.

Valkin the Grand Event

Triggered by talking to Crazy Lou three times. She warns you, then the Event Script Engine launches the Valkin cinematic -- a dramatic encounter with the game's primary antagonist. This event uses the data-driven Event Engine with 17 action types.

CHAPTER XIIDungeons

Frost Dungeon

☠ The First Dungeon

Entrance at overworld position (38, 3). King Jay guards the hallway -- he taunts you before dropping you through a trapdoor into the dungeon proper.

The dungeon consists of an intro hallway, 3 combat rooms, and a boss encounter:

  1. Intro Hallway -- Walk up to King Jay. He delivers a cinematic taunt, then the floor drops away.
  2. Room 1 & 2 -- Clear mob encounters. Enemies at standard HP. Healing between fights (if enabled).
  3. Room 3 (Boss) -- Face Romy, Seer of the Frozen Vale. Boss HP = card max HP x3.

Dungeon Mechanics

  • Aggro on Contact: Walk into a mob = battle starts instantly.
  • Door Locks: Clear all enemies in a room to unlock the next door.
  • Boss HP Multiplier: x3 (the boss is three times as tanky as its card stats).
  • Failure: Lose 15 gold and return to the overworld exit at (38, 5).
  • Victory: 100 gold reward. Return through the hallway (King Jay is gone). Exit south.

CHAPTER XIIIMultiplayer

Player Presence

Firebase-backed real-time presence system. Players appear in the same region and can see each other, chat, trade, and team up. Presence is region-scoped for performance.

Chat System

Region-based chat with a 120-character limit per message. Messages are visible to everyone in your current region.

Raid System

World bosses use Firebase atomic transactions for raid-style multiplayer. Multiple players contribute damage to the same boss simultaneously. A spectator feed shows who hit what and for how much.

PvP & Co-op

  • PvP: Challenge other players to dice battles. Red team vs Blue team. Arena battles earn glory and gold.
  • Co-op: Team up for world bosses and dungeon runs. Party members share XP bonuses (+50% XP while grouped).

Player Market

List items for sale on the player market. Earn the Verified Fighter badge through PvP wins. Tipping system for generous trades. Merchants get better prices and access to advanced market features.

Housing Visits

Visit other players' houses to see their trophy collections. Each house has a guest log.

CHAPTER XVIBuffs & Status Effects

Buff Types

Buffs are tracked per-player with expiration timers. They come from talents, gardens, NPCs, and items. Each buff has an effect object with one or more of these properties:

PropertyEffect
luckyStonesBonus Lucky Stones at battle start
bonusDamageFlat damage bonus to winning rolls
diceModExtra dice added to rolls
healHPHeal on application
speedModMovement speed multiplier
xpModXP gain multiplier
dmgReductionFlat damage reduction from incoming hits

Buffs can be time-based (expires after X minutes) or consumed on battle (one-time use). Some buffs persist through KO (like Timeless Fortune).

Fortune System

Fortune Tellers give fortunes with a 5-second cooldown. Base odds: 50/50 Good/Bad.

  • Good Fortune: +1 Lucky Stone (or +2 with Potent Fortune talent)
  • Bad Fortune: -1 die on first roll (or -2 with Cruel Fortune talent)

Duration scales with talents: 1 battle (base) → 5 min → 20 min → 1 hour. Dark Fortune branch shifts odds to 35/65 Good/Bad.

Notable Buffs

  • Garden Communion -- Shaman meditates at a garden: +1 die for 10-30 min
  • Preparation -- Scholar gives +1 die on first roll (requires tavern)
  • Elder Barrier -- AoE: ward Dark Riders, heal all nearby to full HP
  • Essence Spark -- Enchanter consumes essence for +5% XP buff
  • House Buff -- +25% XP for 2 hours after visiting your house
  • Party Bonus -- +50% XP while grouped with other players
